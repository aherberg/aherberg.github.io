ERA Redesign

New web, mobile, & brand experience.

For this project, I was tasked with the redesign and rebranding for a non-profit client: The Electronic Reusing Association (ERA). My challenge was to create a new web layout and style guide for the organization.

  • Visual / Web Designer

The Client

The Electronic Reusing Association (ERA) is a non-profit organization focused on the reduction of e-waste and providing communities with affordable electronics. They are headquartered in Canada, but have expanded to include locations across the USA.

The ERA‚Äôs vision is to rethink e-waste as an opportunity to recover unwanted IT euiptment and refurbish donated electronics to prepare them for reuse. The refurbished equipment is then donated to organizations and programs within the community.

Web Design

Over the course of 5 weeks I created mutliple iterations for the ERA's web and mobile-responsive website. I would bring my designs to class each week and participate in group design critiques.

Web Layout

Here are a few examples of wireframes I created for the landing page. Feedback from my classmates drove the final concept, which included brighter colors, rounded edges, and displaying information with more prominence, utilizing large font titles and spaces for content.

The final "How it works" page also utilized the design feedback to incorporate visual storytelling. The existing site (pictured on the left) had a lot of buttons that weren't buttons, very small icons, and scattered content, which users felt was busy and confusing in some places. My redesign focused on creating a clear, linear path through all the steps of the donation experience.

Imagery

Throughout this project, I was most proud of the imagery I chose for the website. To truly express the journey of the ERA, I felt that illustrations would best describe and convey those points. For this, I designed and created custom illustrations to align directly with the ERA's messaging rather than opting for stock images.

Channels

To implement my new branding, I incorporated my visual systems into the ERA's website, a mobile responsive website, and a moving van because one of the ERA's primary functions is to pickup and deliver recycled electronics. This channel seemed both appropriate and a way for the ERA to get their brand more visibility.
